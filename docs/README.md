# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management documentation hub. This README provides an overview of OctoAcme's project management framework and links to all supporting process documents.

---

## Overview

OctoAcme's project management framework is designed to provide structure, clarity, and repeatability across all product development endeavors. The lifecycle begins with thorough project initiation, where new ideas are validated through a one-pager that outlines the problem, goal, success metrics, stakeholders, and risks. Upon stakeholder alignment and approval, the planning phase breaks projects into actionable increments, prioritizes work in backlogs, identifies dependencies and risks, and defines clear "Definition of Done" criteria. Workflows leverage the use of project boards to visualize progress (columns like Backlog, Ready, In Progress, QA, Done), ensuring tasks pass through defined stages and bottlenecks are escalated in a structured manner using documented escalation paths from daily team triage to sponsor-level attention for critical blockers.

A clear delineation of roles underpins OctoAcme's approach: Project Managers (PMs) coordinate timelines, manage risks, and facilitate communication; Product Managers (PdMs) own product vision, success metrics, and backlog prioritization; Developers implement, test, and review features; QA personnel validate against acceptance and quality standards; and Stakeholders provide direction and approvals. Each role comes with specific responsibilities and goals, encouraging accountability and minimizing ambiguous ownership. These personas interact through standardized routines such as daily standups, sprint planning, backlog refinement, code reviews, and retrospectives, promoting a culture of psychological safety and iterative improvement.

Communication is at the core of OctoAcme's practices. Weekly syncs between PMs and PdMs, regular standups for the delivery team, and monthly stakeholder updates ensure transparency and rapid alignment. Risks and dependencies are tracked in a living risk register, escalated promptly according to impact and urgency, and communicated through status templates or incident summaries as needed. All documentation and artifacts are centralized, with clear guidance for recording decisions, actions, and retrospectives to ensure knowledge is institutionalized and accessible.

Quality assurance is embedded from planning through release. Teams are expected to prepare thorough test plans, execute automated unit and integration tests, and conduct manual QA for high-impact features. The pull request workflow enforces pre-merge testing, peer review, and acceptance criteria completeness. Releases are governed by pre-release checklists (ensuring all tests and criteria are met), with clear rollback and incident response protocols. Continuous improvement is fostered through structured retrospectives where teams review what worked, identify opportunities, and assign actionable follow-ups, contributing to a robust, adaptable process that supports OctoAcme's commitment to excellence.
